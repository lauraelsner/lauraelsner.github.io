The Wall Street Journal - Responsive Article Template

Role

UX Designer

Launch Date

January, 2015

Project Overview

Re-strategize design and editorial publishing approach to WSJ articles with 4 specific types of storytelling. Improve media insets and ability to package articles more effectively.

Process

  1. Requirements Gathering
  2. Discovery & Research
  3. User Feedback Sessions
  4. Concept Wireframes
  5. Prototypes & flows
  6. Final Wireframes
  7. Responsive Templates & Documentation

Discovery & Research

Omniture data and early user testing indicated that users do not generally navigate to section fronts unless they are a paid WSJ subscriber. Our goal was not necessarily to to "do away with" the section fronts entirely, but to bring the section front experience to non-subscribers and provide a more holistic WSJ experience.

User Feedback Sessions

With other members of the redesign team, I conducted and aggregated data from user feedback sessions based on two types of story-telling: Immersive and standard. Upon completion of the sessions, we set out to explore both variations in greater detail.
